# Financial Risk Factors

## Interest rate risk
Companies with floating-rate debt face higher interest expenses when rates rise. News mentioning "rate exposure," "refinancing risk," or "higher borrowing costs" often carries negative sentiment.

## Credit and default risk
References to covenant breaches, credit downgrades, or liquidity shortages signal elevated default risk. Bond spreads widening and rating agency actions are common triggers.

## Regulatory risk
Changes in financial regulation, antitrust actions, or compliance investigations can negatively affect affected firms. Regulatory fines and consent decrees are typically negative headlines.

## Foreign exchange risk
Multinational firms reporting currency headwinds may see translated earnings decline even when local operations perform well. FX volatility is a recurring risk factor in earnings calls.

## Concentration risk
Heavy reliance on a single customer, product, or geographic market increases vulnerability. Diversification reduces this risk; concentration events (lost contracts, regional slowdowns) are often negative.

## Supply chain disruption
Production delays, input cost spikes, and logistics bottlenecks can compress margins. Post-pandemic supply chain news frequently moved markets and sentiment.

## Competitive pressure
Market share losses, price wars, and disruptive entrants can erode profitability. Language about "intensifying competition" or "pricing pressure" tends toward negative sentiment.

## Macro recession risk
Broad economic slowdowns reduce consumer spending and corporate investment. Recession fears, inverted yield curves, and weak PMI data amplify negative sentiment across sectors.

## ESG and reputational risk
Environmental incidents, governance scandals, and social controversies can trigger sell-offs independent of near-term earnings. Reputational damage may affect brand value and regulatory scrutiny.

## Volatility and market risk
Sharp stock price swings, margin calls, and forced selling increase systemic risk. VIX spikes and correlation breakdowns are indicators of elevated market stress.
