# Financial Terms Glossary

## EBITDA
Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A measure of operating profitability used to compare companies across industries and capital structures.

## EPS (Earnings Per Share)
Net income divided by the number of outstanding shares. Rising EPS often signals improved profitability and is closely watched during earnings season.

## Revenue Growth
Year-over-year or quarter-over-quarter increase in total sales. Consistent revenue growth is a positive indicator of market demand and business expansion.

## Operating Margin
Operating income divided by revenue. Higher margins suggest efficient cost management and pricing power.

## Free Cash Flow (FCF)
Cash generated after capital expenditures. Strong FCF supports dividends, buybacks, and debt reduction.

## Guidance
Management's forward-looking forecast for revenue, earnings, or other metrics. Upward guidance revisions are typically viewed positively; downward revisions negatively.

## Debt-to-Equity Ratio
Total liabilities divided by shareholder equity. High leverage increases financial risk, especially in rising-rate environments.

## Market Sentiment
The overall attitude of investors toward a security or market. Sentiment can be driven by earnings reports, macroeconomic data, and news headlines.

## Bullish vs Bearish
Bullish sentiment expects prices to rise; bearish sentiment expects declines. Financial news language often encodes sentiment through words like "surged," "slumped," or "remained flat."

## Dividend Yield
Annual dividend per share divided by stock price. Dividend cuts or suspensions are generally negative signals for income-focused investors.
